I. Fundamental Analysis

A. Stock Selection Criteria

V40 & V40 Next (Market Leaders):
  • Must be a top 3 leader in its industry by sales/net profit.
  • Minimum 15–20 years in business.
  • Debt-free with strong growth prospects.
  • Pricing power (brand value; avoid utilities/government companies).
  • Market cap categories: Large-cap (Top 100), Mid-cap (101–250), Small-cap (Rest).
V200 (High-Profit Companies):
  • Profit > ₹200 crore in the last 12 months.
  • ROCE > 20%.
  • Debt-equity ratio < 0.25 (exceptions for banks/NBFCs: ROE > 10%).

B. Financial Metrics

  • ROCE (Return on Capital Employed): >15% (ideal: >30%).
  • Debt-Equity Ratio: <0.25 (best: <0.10).
  • Pledging: Avoid if promoters pledge >10% of holdings.
  • Quarterly Results: Compare YoY; ignore short-term dips for strong companies.

C. Business & Market Analysis

  • Focus on companies with low market penetration and high growth potential.
  • Compare peers (e.g., Asian Paints vs. Indigo Paints, Britannia vs. Bector Foods).
  • Avoid government-owned firms and commodity-based businesses (no pricing power).

II. Trading Strategies

1. Simple Moving Average (SMA) Strategy
  • Applicable to: V40 only
  • Objective: Identify trend reversals using moving averages.
  • Setup: Daily candlestick chart with 200-day (Black), 50-day (Red), and 20-day (Green) SMAs.
  • Buy Conditions: 200 SMA > 50 SMA > 20 SMA (Downtrend weakening). Price is at the lowest level of the recent downtrend, with a green candle closing above the 20 SMA.
  • Sell Conditions: 200 SMA < 50 SMA < 20 SMA (Uptrend weakening). Price is at the highest level of the recent uptrend, with a red candle closing below the 20 SMA.
  • Risk Management: 3% of portfolio per trade. No averaging. No stop loss (relies on V40 company strength).
2. Knoxville Strategy : Use Trading View
  • Applicable to: V40 only
  • Objective: Trade based on trendline breakouts.
  • Setup: RB_KnoxDiv indicator (Bars Back: 200, RSI: 14, Momentum: 20).
  • Buy Condition: Price breaks the downtrend line. Enter at next day's opening price.
  • Sell Condition: Price breaks the uptrend line. Exit at next day's opening price.
  • Risk Management: 3% of portfolio. Averaging allowed if the second trade is 5% lower. No stop loss.
3. V20 Strategy
  • Applicable to: V40, V40 Next, V200
  • Objective: Trade within a 20% price range.
  • Setup: Identify a 20%+ price swing and mark the top (resistance) and bottom (support).
  • Buy Condition: Price falls below the range low.
  • Sell Condition: Price reaches the top of the range.
  • Risk Management: 3% per trade (max 9% per stock). Averaging allowed at 10% lower than the previous entry.
4. Reverse Head & Shoulders (RHS) / Cup & Handle
  • Applicable to: V40 & V40 Next
  • Objective: Trade breakout patterns with high reward potential.
  • RHS Rules: Flat neckline. Shoulders not deeper than the head. Buy on a green candle breakout above the neckline with increased volume.
  • Cup & Handle Rules: Handle must be smaller than the cup. Buy on breakout above handle's resistance.
  • Target: Measure depth from head/cup to neckline and project upwards. Minimum 40% gain.
  • Risk Management: 3% of portfolio. No stop loss.
5. Lifetime High Strategy
  • Applicable to: V40 & V40 Next
  • Objective: Buy strong companies at a 30% discount to lifetime highs.
  • Buy Conditions: Company has lifetime high TTM sales & profits, but the price is 30% below its lifetime high.
  • Averaging Rules: Add another 3% if the price drops another 10%. Max allocation of 10% per stock.
  • Exit Condition: Sell at the lifetime high.
6. Contrarian Strategy ("3 Times in 3 Years")
  • Applicable to: All Stocks
  • Objective: Buy oversold stocks with recovery potential.
  • Buy Conditions: Price is down ≥67% from lifetime high due to a temporary reason. Business is improving, and the price is still ≤50% below its lifetime high.
  • Exit Rules: Sell after a 100% gain within 12 months OR hold until the lifetime high is reached.
